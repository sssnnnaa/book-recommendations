  • How to Win Friends and Influence People

    Dale Carnegie’s classic guide on communication and persuasion, teaching how to build strong relationships, win others over, and handle conflicts effectively.

    Rich Dad Poor Dad

    Robert Kiyosaki shares how rich people make money work for them instead of working for money, challenging conventional wisdom about money and explaining the difference between assets and liabilities.

    Good to Great

    Jim Collins examines why some companies make the leap from good to great while others don't, identifying key determinants of greatness including Level 5 Leadership and the Hedgehog Concept.

    The Psychology of Money

    Morgan Housel explores the strange ways people think about money and teaches you how to make better sense of one of life's most important topics, through 19 short stories exploring the unique ways people think about money.

    The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People

    Stephen Covey presents a holistic, integrated approach for solving personal and professional problems by revealing a step-by-step pathway for living with fairness, integrity, honesty, and human dignity.

    Atomic Habits

    James Clear reveals practical strategies that will teach you how to form good habits, break bad ones, and master the tiny behaviors that lead to remarkable results through his proven framework for improving every day.

    The Intelligent Investor

    Benjamin Graham’s classic guide to value investing, teaching principles of risk management, long-term thinking, and disciplined decision-making for financial success.

    Think and Grow Rich

    Napoleon Hill’s timeless guide to success, outlining key principles like desire, persistence, and mindset to achieve wealth and personal fulfillment.

    Start with Why

    Simon Sinek reveals that the world's most influential leaders all think, act, and communicate in the same way - they all start with WHY. Discover how to create lasting success by inspiring others to buy into your vision.

    Predictably Irrational

    Dan Ariely challenges our assumptions about making decisions based on rational thought, revealing the fundamental predictability of our irrational behavior and how it affects our daily lives.

    Zero to One

    Peter Thiel shows how we can find singular ways to create new things, arguing that progress comes from learning to think for yourself and exploring uncharted frontiers rather than competing in crowded markets.

    Mindset

    Carol S. Dweck reveals how success in school, work, sports, the arts, and almost every area of human endeavor can be dramatically influenced by how we think about our talents and abilities.
